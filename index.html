Skilld

I was contacted by Skilld, an Australian employment portal, to copywrite a landing page for their new service, developed in partnership with the Chamber of Commerce and Industry of Queensland (CCIQ).

The project was very challenging in that no organized information was provided upfront. As such, my first task was to research Skilld’s proposed new service and highlight the features most appealing to their existing customer base.

I ended up with three categories of features, and organized the offer with these as an overall framework. Skilld agreed with my choices, and the project proceeded smoothly with two more straightforward drafts.

After being very happy with the final result, they have agreed to let me showcase my work. Keep in mind that the version you are reading is undesigned and contains only the finished text.

Psycknowlogy

“Carl did great work. He was responsive to feedback and easy to communicate with.”
- Allison Dempsey, Psycknowlogy



Two professors of Pediatrics from the University of Texas needed a document describing their latest invention - an interactive digital screening system for detecting developmental delays in children under 5 years of age. The ultimate goal was to receive investment and support from their university, and to bring the invention to market.

The hardest part of this project was to keep it short. I was given a wealth of information, statistics and relevant arguments to choose from - but the final document had to be 1 page. I also had to make room for two logos, six small pictures, and two portraits. Yikes.

I ended up with four short sections - An overview of the problem, a description of the invention, an analysis of its market potential, and how development would proceed with further funding.

Confidentiality forbids me from discussing any details, but suffice to say that the professors were left satisfied.

Blueflower

“Carl is a pleasure to work with - meticulous, willing to work on reviewing the product until perfect. Total customer focus. Thank you!”
- Andrea Oschetti, BlueFlower

I was approached by BlueFlower, a luxury travel agency, who needed me to write short and enticing ads for their trips to Antarctica, The Carnival of Venice and The Dolomites.

To start with, there was very little information on the trips available for me to work with. My first initiative was therefore to interview the organizers of each trip, to properly understand exactly what each trip contained.

Thus armed, I wrote the ads according to a specific set of requirements - Title, two introductory sentences, a few bulletpoints and an upsell at the end.

Repeat work is the best feedback a freelancer can get - that’s why I was thrilled when an opportunity to write all their ads presented itself.
